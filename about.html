From humble beginnings...

Beginning in a one-car garage in 1934, J.R. Fitzgerald founded Tri-Pak Machinery after years of working in fruit and vegetable packing sheds across South Texas. His experiences in those packing sheds led him to develop machinery solutions that created efficiency and enhanced profitability in the processing and packaging of fresh produce.

Tri-Pak quickly expanded to its current 4.75-acre location in 1939 where the company has continued to develop high quality custom machinery based on the pioneering efforts of their founder.

His first invention, a sorting conveyor for tomatoes, is just one example of the types of innovative problem solving that has been at the core of Tri-Pak's culture since the very beginning.

Where tomatoes were previously being individually hand wrapped in tissue paper to prevent damage during transit, he discovered that they could be lubricated with a thin film of food-grade wax for the same effect with less packaging waste.

Utilizing a conveyor system also allowed for additional modular machinery, leading to the development of an all-inclusive packing line that served to wash, dry, wax, sort by size, grade by ripeness, and pack into a finished container ready to ship to the local grocers.

Tri-Pak is presently being managed by the third generation of Fitzgeralds: David, Jim, and Bobby and the business has expanded to include the custom design and manufacture of equipment for packing onions, carrots, potatoes, cucumbers, citrus fruit, cabbage, shrimp and other commodities. The most modern technology has continued to be incorporated in the manufacturing process and the firm's reputation for quality, dependability and service has grown worldwide. Tri-Pak’s reputation for excellence is built on J.R.’s philosophy that the pillars of quality, quantity and uniformity are integral to creating a lasting product and a satisfied customer.
